"I Feel Blank"- A Game about Emotional Awareness in the Decision-Making Process

About this Site

This site is a prototype of the game "I Feel Blank", which was created as part of a Final project for SS142 coursework at Minerve University. This website is meant to serve as a digital gameboard and guide for anyone to play "I Feel Blank" with a friend. The game runs locally, so players are meant to take turns on the same computer, as if sharing a physical gameboard.

Controls

During the Setup Phase, Click on each Token to select them and move each Player to their chosen starting position.

Use the Arrow Keys to Move and Shift/Ctrl and Rotate clockwise/counterclockwise, respectively. If you make a mistake on your Turn, Backspace will undo your last move.

Once ready, press Enter to move on to the next Phase of the game, and press R to restart the game.

You may always Hover over a Token to see its current position.


Full Game Ruleset

  • EMOTION MAPS:
    • Each Player assigns an emotion to each of their Token's 8 possible positions on the game board; this constitutes their Emotion Map
    • There are 8 board positions, one for each orientation-and-color configuration a Player's Token can take on the game board
      • Blue-North ⇧, Blue-South ⇩, Blue-East ⇨, Blue-West ⇦
      • Green-North ⇧, Green-South ⇩, Green-East ⇨, Green-West ⇦

  • OBJECTIVE:
    • Win by correctly guessing your opponent's complete Emotion Map

  • PROMPTS:
    • During gameplay, Prompts provide cues and/or context for Players' emotional Responses
    • A list of example prompts is included, but you're encouraged to come up with your own

  • RESPONSES TO PROMPTS:
    • When a Prompt is presented, one or both Players may have to Respond to it by providing a short (1-2 sentences) description of their reaction to the Prompt, based on the Emotion assigned to the Player's position in their Emotion Map
    • Responses can be Sincere (based on the emotion mapped to the Player's current position on their Emotion map) or Deceptive (based on any emotion not mapped to the Player's current position on their Emotion Map)

  • SETUP:
    1. Choose tokens
    2. Fill out Emotion Maps by assigning emotions to board positions
    3. Choose starting positions
    4. Share the 8 emotions each Player has chosen, but not their assigned positions
    5. Flip a coin to decide who starts

  • PLAYING THE GAME:
    • Every Round consists of a Turn for each Player and one Insight Phase
    • A Player Turn consists of
      1. A Move, where the Player chooses to either Jump to an adjacent square or Rotate 90° clockwise/counterclockwise, but not both
      2. A Scenario, the format of which depends on the state of the board after the Player's Move:
        1. If neither of the Players faces the other, the Turn ends
        2. If both Players face each other, then a Prompt is chosen at Random and both Players must Respond to it Sincerely
        3. If one Player (e.g. Green) faces the side of the other (Blue), then that Player (Green) chooses a Prompt that the other (Blue) must Respond to Sincerely
        4. If one Player (e.g. Green) faces the back of the other (Blue), then that Player (Green) chooses a Prompt that the other (Blue) must Respond to Deceptively
    • During the Insight Phase, which happens at the end of a Round, after each Player completed their Turn, both Players have the opportunity to Guess the position of one emotion in their opponent's Emotion Map and get a truthful Yes/No answer (one or both may choose not to guess).

  • RESTRICTIONS:
    • You must Move on your turn, you cannot remain in the same position
    • You cannot take consecutive opposite Moves to "undo" a Turn; you can't Jump or Rotate in one direction and then back on your next Turn
